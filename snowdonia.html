Snowdonia National Park

Snowdonia National Park (Which in Welsh is Eryri or "Place of eagles") is an area of outstanding natural beauty which covers 838 square miles! The highest peak in the area is Snowdon (Yr Wyddfa) which is 3560ft or 1085m, it's also the higest mountain in England and Wales!
There is also a number of well-established walking routes to allow access to it's summit. There is also a rack and pinion railway that begins it's journey in Llanberis and snakes its way through the foothills to the summit

There are a number of other spectacular peaks in the area that exceed 3000ft including...

  • Carnedd Dafydd
  • Carnedd Llewelyn
  • Castell y Gwynt
  • Crib Coch
  • Crib y Ddysgl
  • Elidir Fawr
  • Foel Grach
  • Foel-fras
  • Garnedd Uchaf
  • Glyder Fach
  • Glyder Fawr
  • Pen yr Ole Wen
  • Tryfan
  • Y Garn
  • Yr Elen
